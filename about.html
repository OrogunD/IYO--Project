THE PIANO

According to Wikipedia Contributors, 2022, The word "Piano" is a shortened of the word pianoforte, the italian term for early 1700s versions of the instrument, the word stems from clavicembalo col piano e forte(key cimbalom with quiet and loud) and fortepiano. The italian musical terms piano and fort mean "soft" and "loud," respectively and refer to the variations in volume considered to be due to the physical contact made by a pianist's on the keys the harder the pianist strikes the key the greater the force of the hammer hitting the strings, hence making the notes louder.
Most current pianos feature an 88-key row with 52 white keys for the notes of the C major scale (C, D, E, F, G, A, and B) and 36 shorter black keys elevated above the white keys and situated further back on the keyboard.
The invention of the piano is credited to Bartolomeo Cristofori (1655–1731) of Padua. since then the piano has been such a magical tool of music, why don't you click the button below to find out more about the piano!!
